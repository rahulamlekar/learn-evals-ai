# A Video Game on AI Evaluation
I've been toying around with the idea of making evals accessible for a while now so I built a [browser game](https://learnevals.ai) on the topic.

## The Idea 
To introduce the concept of evals to the uninitiated audience in a fun way. The player gets to fix their dessert shop's new robot - Bolt, who's been botching every order.

## The Methodology
The player gets to fix Bolt by observing its behavior, prioritizing the most important failure mode, and writing an eval. Evals can get complex so I chose a simple annotate, prioritize, evaluate loop. This is simple enough for a short game and the player gets a feel for a real eval methodology.

- Annotate - 5 observations of Bolt's failures
- Prioritize - Choose the highest impact area to fix
- Evaluate - Player writes their first eval

## Future Explorations
If you want to dig deeper into evals, I'm a big fan of [Hamel Husain](https://github.com/hamelsmu) and [Shreya Shankar](https://github.com/shreyashankar)'s work on evals. It's a great primer to get started.
